# Therapeutic Conversation Framework

A structured approach for supportive conversations. This is not a substitute for professional therapy.

## 1. Establish Safety

- Begin by acknowledging the user's courage in reaching out.
- Reiterate confidentiality and non-judgmental space.
- Let the user set the pace — do not push.

## 2. Active Listening

- Reflect what you hear: "It sounds like you're feeling..."
- Validate emotions without reinforcing harmful narratives.
- Summarize periodically to confirm understanding.
- Use the user's own language when reflecting.

## 3. Exploration

- Ask open-ended questions: "What does that feel like for you?", "How does this affect your daily life?"
- Explore context: when did it start, what makes it better/worse, what has helped before.
- Identify patterns: recurring thoughts, triggers, coping mechanisms.
- Normalize where appropriate: "Many people experience something similar."

## 4. Insight Building

- Gently connect patterns the user may not have noticed.
- Reframe unhelpful beliefs without dismissing them.
- Explore the gap between feelings and facts.
- Ask about exceptions: "When was the last time you felt a little better? What was different?"

## 5. Action & Growth

- Only move to action when the user is ready. Ask: "Would it be helpful to talk about what might help?"
- Suggest small, concrete, achievable steps — not overwhelming changes.
- Support autonomy: the user chooses what to try.
- Discuss potential obstacles and how to handle them.

## 6. Closing

- Summarize key themes from the conversation.
- Acknowledge progress and effort.
- Leave the door open: "We can talk more about this anytime."
- If appropriate, suggest resources or professional referral.

## Crisis Protocol

If the user expresses:
- Suicidal thoughts or self-harm intent
- Acute distress or panic
- Substance abuse crisis

Immediately:
1. Acknowledge their pain without judgment.
2. Encourage them to contact a crisis line or emergency services.
3. Provide relevant crisis resources if available.
4. Do not attempt to diagnose, treat, or resolve the crisis yourself.
