# Research Methodology Designer

Use this skill when the user wants to design an experiment, evaluate a study plan, or structure a research question.

## Goal

Translate a scientific question into a rigorous, actionable study design.

## Design framework

Structure every study using these components:

1. **Research question** — What do you want to know? Is it descriptive, relational, or causal?
2. **Hypothesis** — A falsifiable statement predicting an outcome.
3. **Prediction** — The expected observation if the hypothesis is true.
4. **Variables**
   - Independent / predictor variables.
   - Dependent / outcome variables.
   - Confounders to control or measure.
5. **Study design** — Choose among RCT, cohort, case-control, cross-sectional, natural experiment, modeling, qualitative, etc., with justification.
6. **Sampling and power** — Target population, sampling method, inclusion/exclusion criteria, and rough sample-size rationale.
7. **Data collection** — Instruments, protocols, blinding, and controls.
8. **Analysis plan** — Statistical tests or models, significance thresholds, handling of missing data, and pre-registration.
9. **Interpretation criteria** — What result would support, refute, or leave the hypothesis uncertain?
10. **Limitations** — Internal validity, external validity, ethical constraints, resource limits.
11. **Next steps** — Replication, follow-up studies, or practical applications.

## Steps

1. **Clarify the question** — Move from vague curiosity to a specific, answerable question.
2. **Select the design** — Match the design to the question and constraints.
3. **Anticipate threats** — List the main biases and how to mitigate them.
4. **Draft the protocol** — Lay out the framework above in order.
5. **Review feasibility** — Time, cost, ethics, access, and expertise.

## Output format

- **Question and hypothesis**.
- **Design choice** with justification.
- **Key variables and controls**.
- **Procedure outline**.
- **Analysis plan**.
- **Interpretation and limitations**.
- **Next steps**.

## Boundaries

- Do not fabricate data or guarantee results.
- Do not skip ethics review for human or animal research.
- Flag when a question requires specialized expertise (e.g., clinical trials, structural engineering) beyond general methodology.
