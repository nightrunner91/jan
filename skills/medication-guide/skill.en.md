# Medication Guide

Use this skill when the user asks about a drug, needs prescribing guidance, or wants to compare treatment options.

## Goal

Give precise, safe, and structured medication information that supports informed prescribing and patient counseling.

## Steps

1. **Identify the drug and context** — active ingredient, formulation, indication, patient population (age, pregnancy, renal/hepatic function, comorbidities).
2. **Explain mechanism of action** — how the drug works in plain language.
3. **List indications** — approved and common off-label uses, if relevant.
4. **Provide dosing** — standard adult dosing, route, frequency, duration, and adjustments for special populations.
5. **Describe the typical course** — when to expect effect, titration schedule, and stopping rules.
6. **List common and serious side effects** — distinguish frequent mild effects from rare but dangerous ones.
7. **State contraindications and precautions** — pregnancy/lactation, age, organ impairment, relevant comorbidities.
8. **Check interactions** — drug-drug, drug-food, drug-alcohol, and any relevant pharmacogenetic considerations.
9. **Add monitoring** — labs, vitals, or clinical checks needed during therapy.
10. **Cite sources** — guidelines, formularies, or primary literature.

## Output format

- **Drug**: name and formulation.
- **Mechanism**: brief mechanism of action.
- **Indications**: what it is used for.
- **Dosage & course**: dose, route, frequency, duration, adjustments.
- **Side effects**: common | serious.
- **Contraindications & precautions**: key warnings.
- **Interactions**: clinically significant ones.
- **Monitoring**: required follow-up.
- **References**: citations or authoritative sources.

## Boundaries

- Do not recommend medications without considering contraindications and interactions.
- Do not provide pediatric, pregnancy, or geriatric dosing without explicit safety checks.
- Always remind the user that final prescribing decisions belong to a licensed clinician.
