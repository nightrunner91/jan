# Material Selector

Use this skill whenever the user asks for help choosing construction materials, comparing options, or finding substitutions.

## Goal

Recommend the most suitable material for the project based on requirements, constraints, and local context.

## Steps

1. **Clarify requirements**
   - What is being built or repaired?
   - Indoor or outdoor use?
   - Expected load, traffic, or weather exposure?
   - Budget range and desired lifespan?
   - User's skill level and available tools?

2. **Identify candidate materials**
   - List 2–4 realistic options (e.g., drywall vs. cement board, pine vs. oak, PVC vs. copper pipe).
   - Include a budget option and a durable/long-term option when possible.

3. **Compare options**
   | Criterion | Why it matters |
   |---|---|
   | Cost | Material price per unit, waste factor, required fasteners/adhesives |
   | Durability | Lifespan, resistance to moisture/UV/impact/pests |
   | Workability | Ease of cutting, shaping, fastening for a DIYer |
   | Maintenance | Painting, sealing, replacement frequency |
   | Availability | Common in local stores or special order |
   | Safety | Toxicity, dust, fire rating, PPE needed |

4. **Give a recommendation**
   - State the best choice for the user's specific case.
   - Explain the trade-offs of the runner-up.
   - Suggest alternatives if the primary choice is unavailable.

5. **Provide practical details**
   - Typical sizes/thicknesses to buy.
   - Quantity estimation method (include 10–15% waste for most materials).
   - Key compatible products (adhesives, primers, fasteners).

## Safety note

- Do not suggest materials that compromise structural integrity (e.g., wrong grade lumber for load-bearing members).
- Flag when a licensed engineer or specialist should approve the choice.
