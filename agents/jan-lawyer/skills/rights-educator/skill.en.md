# Rights Educator

Use this skill when the user asks about their legal rights in a specific context, such as housing, employment, consumer transactions, family matters, or dealings with public authorities.

## Goal

Explain what rights Moldovan law generally provides in a given situation, what practical limitations exist, and how a person can exercise or protect those rights.

## Steps

1. **Identify the context** — tenant/landlord, employee/employer, consumer/business, family, public services, etc.
2. **State the relevant legal framework** — Moldovan codes, laws, or regulations that apply in general terms.
3. **List key rights** — what the law grants to the person in that role.
4. **List corresponding obligations** — duties the person also has, to keep the picture balanced.
5. **Explain practical limitations** — what may reduce rights in practice: contracts, deadlines, proof requirements, enforcement gaps.
6. **Describe how to exercise rights** — steps, documents, and the competent authority or court.
7. **Provide remedies** — what the person can do if their rights are violated: complaints, claims, penalties.
8. **Add real-life examples** — short, relatable scenarios that illustrate the rules.
9. **Conclude with a disclaimer** — information is general and not a substitute for personalized legal advice.

## Output format

- **Context**: the situation or role.
- **Applicable law**: Moldovan statutes in general terms.
- **Your rights**: key entitlements.
- **Your obligations**: corresponding duties.
- **Practical limitations**: what can weaken or complicate rights.
- **How to exercise**: concrete steps.
- **Remedies**: options if rights are violated.
- **Examples**: brief scenarios.
- **Disclaimer**: consult a licensed attorney for specific cases.

## Boundaries

- Do not encourage confrontation or unlawful self-help remedies.
- Do not present rights as absolute; always note conditions and exceptions.
- Stay within Moldovan jurisdiction unless the user indicates otherwise.
