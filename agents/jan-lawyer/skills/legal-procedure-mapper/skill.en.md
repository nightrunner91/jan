# Legal Procedure Mapper

Use this skill when the user asks how to complete a legal or administrative procedure in Moldova, such as filing a claim, registering property, obtaining a certificate, or appealing a decision.

## Goal

Give a clear, actionable roadmap for legal procedures under the Republic of Moldova, including steps, documents, timelines, fees, and common pitfalls.

## Steps

1. **Identify the procedure** — confirm what the user wants to accomplish and the legal area involved.
2. **Determine the competent body** — court, public authority, notary, registry, or other institution.
3. **List prerequisites** — legal standing, deadlines, prior steps, mandatory notifications, or attempts at settlement.
4. **Lay out the steps** — numbered chronological actions the user must take.
5. **Specify required documents** — forms, IDs, proof of address, evidence, powers of attorney, translations, apostilles.
6. **State timelines** — statutory deadlines, expected processing time, and appeal windows.
7. **Mention fees** — state duties, notary fees, service fees where relevant.
8. **Highlight common pitfalls** — missing deadlines, incorrect jurisdiction, insufficient evidence, form errors.
9. **Add next-step options** — appeals, enforcement, or alternatives such as mediation.
10. **Cite legal basis** — relevant Moldovan codes, laws, or regulations in general terms.

## Output format

- **Procedure name**: what the user wants to do.
- **Competent authority**: where to go.
- **Prerequisites**: what is required before starting.
- **Step-by-step actions**: numbered list.
- **Required documents**: checklist.
- **Timelines and deadlines**: statutory and practical.
- **Fees**: approximate costs if known.
- **Common pitfalls**: key mistakes to avoid.
- **Next steps / appeals**: what happens after completion or rejection.
- **Legal basis**: Moldovan statutes or regulations in general terms.

## Boundaries

- Do not guarantee outcomes or processing times; administrative practice may vary.
- Do not provide instructions for bypassing mandatory legal steps.
- Always recommend verifying current rules with the relevant authority or a licensed attorney.
