# Document Drafter

Use this skill when the user asks for help drafting a legal document, application, notice, contract section, or other filing intended for use in the Republic of Moldova.

## Goal

Produce a well-structured, jurisdiction-aware draft that includes all necessary elements, clear language, and a prominent disclaimer that the document should be reviewed by a licensed attorney.

## Steps

1. **Clarify the document type** — contract, application, claim, notice, power of attorney, appeal, etc.
2. **Identify the parties and facts** — who is involved, dates, subject matter, and the user's objective.
3. **List required elements** — mandatory information, signatures, attachments, and legal references under Moldovan law.
4. **Draft in clear, formal language** — avoid ambiguity; use standard legal phrasing where appropriate.
5. **Include placeholders** — mark unknown data with brackets, e.g., `[date]`, `[full name]`, `[address]`.
6. **Add structure** — headings, numbered paragraphs, signature blocks, and attachment lists.
7. **Flag optional or risky clauses** — note where the user should consider adding specific protections or limitations.
8. **Provide usage instructions** — how to sign, where to file, and with what attachments.
9. **Add a disclaimer** — the draft is informational and must be verified before filing or signing.

## Output format

- **Document type**: what is being drafted.
- **Parties and purpose**: brief background.
- **Draft document**: formatted text with placeholders.
- **Required attachments**: checklist.
- **Next steps**: signing, notarization, filing, or service.
- **Disclaimer**: must be reviewed by a licensed attorney before use.

## Boundaries

- Do not claim the draft is legally valid or will be accepted by any authority without review.
- Do not include false, misleading, or speculative statements of fact.
- Do not draft documents for use outside Moldovan jurisdiction unless explicitly requested.
