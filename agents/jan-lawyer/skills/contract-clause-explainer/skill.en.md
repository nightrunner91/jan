# Contract Clause Explainer

Use this skill when the user asks about a specific contract clause, term, or provision, or wants to understand the risks and implications of contract language.

## Goal

Break down contract language into clear, accurate explanations that a non-lawyer can understand, while preserving legal precision and highlighting Moldovan legal context.

## Steps

1. **Identify the clause type** — e.g., termination, liability, confidentiality, payment, force majeure, dispute resolution, governing law.
2. **Extract the exact wording** — quote the clause or key portion if provided by the user.
3. **Explain in plain language** — state what the clause means in practical terms.
4. **Map rights and obligations** — who must do what, when, and under what conditions.
5. **Flag risks and imbalances** — one-sided terms, unclear wording, missing protections, or potential enforceability issues under Moldovan law.
6. **Suggest alternatives or clarifications** — propose neutral wording or questions the user should ask the counterparty.
7. **Add jurisdiction notes** — reference relevant Moldovan statutes or general principles where applicable.
8. **End with a disclaimer** — this is informational, not legal advice.

## Output format

- **Clause type**: category of the provision.
- **Plain-language meaning**: what the clause says in everyday terms.
- **Your rights**: what the clause grants the user.
- **Your obligations**: what the clause requires from the user.
- **Risks / red flags**: potential problems or imbalances.
- **Suggested questions / changes**: points to clarify or negotiate.
- **Moldovan law context**: relevant statutes or general legal principles.
- **Disclaimer**: reminder to consult a licensed attorney for binding advice.

## Boundaries

- Do not state that a clause is definitely enforceable or unenforceable without noting that courts decide this case by case.
- Do not rewrite the entire contract unless explicitly asked; focus on the clause in question.
- Always remain within Moldovan jurisdiction unless the user specifies another applicable law.
