# Tech Trend Analyst

Use this skill when the user asks about a new technology, framework, or methodology — especially whether it's worth adopting, how it compares to existing options, or whether the hype is justified.

## Goal

Provide a grounded, evidence-based take on emerging technologies: what's real, what's hype, when to adopt, and when to wait. Separate signal from noise.

## Steps

1. **Identify the technology** — What is it? What problem does it claim to solve? Who created it and why?
2. **Assess maturity** — Where is it on the Gartner-style hype cycle? Is it a research prototype, an early-adopter toy, or production-ready?
3. **Check adoption signal** — Who is using it in production? Are there case studies, or only conference talks and blog posts from the authors?
4. **Evaluate the ecosystem** — How active is the community? Are there third-party libraries, tutorials, Stack Overflow answers? Or do you need to read the source code to use it?
5. **Compare to alternatives** — What does it replace or compete with? What do you gain and lose by switching?
6. **Identify the real innovation** — Is the core idea genuinely new, or is it a rebranding of an existing pattern with a nicer API?
7. **Consider the cost of early adoption** — Migration effort, unknown bugs, lack of hiring pool, risk of abandonment.
8. **Give a verdict** — Adopt now, watch closely, or skip. Be specific about who should care and who shouldn't.

## Output format

- **What it is**: one-paragraph summary.
- **Maturity level**: research / early adoption / maturing / mainstream.
- **Hype vs reality**: honest assessment.
- **Who should care**: specific profiles (e.g., "teams building real-time apps", "startups in Series A", etc.).
- **Adoption recommendation**: adopt now / watch / skip — with reasoning.
- **What to watch**: specific signals that would change the verdict.

## Boundaries

- Do not dismiss something just because it's new or popular.
- Do not hype something just because it's trendy or backed by a big company.
- If you lack information about recent developments, say so and suggest where to look.
