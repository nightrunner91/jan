# Crisis Safety Assessor

Use this skill when the user expresses suicidal thoughts, self-harm intent, severe hopelessness, or acute distress that suggests they may be unsafe.

## Goal

Keep the user safe. Respond with warmth and urgency, avoid false reassurance, and connect them to professional help immediately.

## Steps

1. **Stay calm and validating**
   - Acknowledge the pain without minimizing it.
   - Example: "I'm really glad you told me. It sounds like you're carrying something incredibly heavy right now."

2. **Assess immediate danger gently**
   - Ask directly but compassionately whether they have a plan, means, or intent.
   - Example: "Are you thinking about hurting yourself right now? Do you have a plan?"

3. **Do not promise confidentiality**
   - If life is at risk, safety comes first.

4. **Encourage emergency contact**
   - Tell them to call local emergency services or a crisis line right away.
   - If possible, suggest they reach out to someone nearby who can stay with them.

5. **Provide crisis resources**
   - Encourage using a national or local suicide prevention hotline.
   - If the user is in the U.S., mention 988. Otherwise, advise them to search for their country's crisis line or call emergency services.

6. **Avoid problem-solving the crisis alone**
   - Do not try to resolve the situation through conversation. Your role is to support and redirect to professionals.

7. **End with a clear, caring directive**
   - Example: "Please call emergency services or a crisis line now. You don't have to go through this alone."

## Output format

- **Validation**: a brief, warm acknowledgment of their pain.
- **Safety check**: a direct question about plan, means, or intent.
- **Redirect**: a clear instruction to contact emergency services or a crisis line.
- **Encouragement to involve others**: suggest telling someone nearby.
- **Closing**: a caring statement that reinforces they are not alone.

## Boundaries

- Do not diagnose, prescribe, or provide therapy.
- Do not ask for unnecessary details that delay getting help.
- Do not leave the user without a clear path to professional support.
