# Warrant & Search Explainer

Use this skill when the user asks about police searches of a home, vehicle, or person, or about warrants and consent in Moldova.

## Goal

Explain the legal framework around searches, distinguish authority from best practice, and tell the user how to behave safely and lawfully.

## Steps

1. **Identify what is being searched** — home, vehicle, person, workplace, belongings.
2. **Explain the warrant requirement** — when a warrant is generally required, what it should contain, and when exceptions may apply.
3. **Clarify consent** — that a person may consent to a search, but can also decline politely; explain practical consequences of refusing.
4. **Describe special circumstances** — searches during arrest, plain-view evidence, emergency exceptions, and border checks.
5. **Outline user rights** — observe and remember details, ask for identification, request a copy of the warrant if one exists, remain calm, avoid physical resistance.
6. **Provide safe behavior guidance** — do not obstruct, do not destroy evidence, ask questions respectfully, document afterward.
7. **Add Moldova-specific practical notes** — typical procedures, who can conduct searches, and where to file a complaint if rights are violated.
8. **Direct to official channels** — explain how to report misconduct or appeal through official bodies.

## Output format

- **Search type**: what is being searched.
- **Warrant rule**: when a warrant is needed and what it must include.
- **Consent**: what consent means and how to refuse.
- **Special circumstances**: brief exceptions.
- **Your rights**: what you can ask or do.
- **Safe behavior**: how to avoid escalation.
- **Moldova notes**: local practical context.
- **Complaint path**: official channels for misconduct or appeals.
- **Disclaimer**: general information, not legal advice.

## Boundaries

- Do not advise destroying evidence, physically resisting officers, lying, or hiding suspects.
- Do not present exceptions as rules or vice versa.
- Do not encourage illegal recording or obstruction.
