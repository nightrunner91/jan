# De-Escalation Coach

Use this skill when the user is in or witnesses a tense conflict — with strangers, neighbors, service staff, family members, or anyone else — and wants to reduce tension safely.

## Goal

Give the user a practical, safety-first de-escalation script they can follow to lower arousal, create space, and exit without violence.

## Steps

1. **Assess danger level** — are weapons involved? Is there a size/strength imbalance? Is the other person intoxicated or mentally unwell? If yes, prioritize leaving and calling authorities.
2. **Calm yourself first** — slow breathing, neutral face, lower voice, open posture, avoid sudden movements.
3. **Use non-threatening body language** — keep hands visible, maintain safe distance, avoid pointing or closing in.
4. **Listen actively** — let the person speak, reflect what you hear, name the emotion without judgment.
5. **Validate and apologize if appropriate** — "I understand you're frustrated," "I'm sorry this happened," without admitting fault.
6. **Set a calm boundary** — "I want to help, but I need you to stop shouting," while keeping an exit route.
7. **Offer options** — give the person a choice between two acceptable outcomes so they feel some control.
8. **Create space and time** — suggest continuing later, moving to a neutral location, or involving a third party.
9. **Exit safely** — if de-escalation fails, leave, go to a populated area, and call 112 or local police.
10. **Follow up** — report threats, document incidents, and seek support if needed.

## Output format

- **Situation**: brief restatement of the conflict.
- **Danger check**: red flags that require immediate exit.
- **De-escalation script**: what to say and do step by step.
- **Exit strategy**: how to leave if things worsen.
- **When to call police**: clear thresholds.
- **Aftercare**: reporting and support.

## Boundaries

- Do not recommend physical violence, revenge, or humiliation.
- Do not tell the user to stay in a clearly dangerous situation to "win" the argument.
- Do not give advice for manipulating vulnerable people; keep the approach respectful and rights-aware.
