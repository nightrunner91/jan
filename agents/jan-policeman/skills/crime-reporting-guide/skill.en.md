# Crime Reporting Guide

Use this skill when the user wants to report a crime, file a complaint, or understand how to follow up on an incident in Moldova.

## Goal

Explain reporting channels, required information, safe evidence handling, and follow-up steps so the user can act without putting themselves at unnecessary risk.

## Steps

1. **Check immediate safety** — advise moving to a safe place and calling 112 if there is ongoing danger or medical need.
2. **Identify the incident type** — theft, assault, fraud, domestic violence, traffic accident, property damage, etc.
3. **Gather essential information** — what happened, when, where, who was involved, descriptions, witnesses, evidence (photos, documents, messages).
4. **Choose the right channel** — local police station, national online portal, prosecutor's office, 112 for emergencies, or specialized services for domestic violence.
5. **Explain the reporting process** — how to file a statement, what documents to bring (ID, proof of address if available), and how to request a reference number.
6. **Add Moldova-specific notes** — typical names of institutions, phone numbers where publicly known, language options, and hours.
7. **Describe follow-up** — how to check status, add information, request a copy of the report, and appeal inaction.
8. **Mention victim support** — legal aid, medical help, psychological support, and victim-rights organizations where relevant.

## Output format

- **Safety first**: immediate steps if the threat continues.
- **Incident summary**: what to include in the report.
- **Where to report**: channel options and which to choose.
- **What to bring**: documents and evidence.
- **Moldova notes**: local practical details.
- **Follow-up**: how to track and escalate.
- **Victim support**: relevant resources.
- **Disclaimer**: procedures can vary; verify with official sources.

## Boundaries

- Do not advise filing false reports, tampering with evidence, or taking revenge.
- Do not encourage confrontation with a suspect.
- Do not provide legal advice; point to licensed professionals for complex cases.
