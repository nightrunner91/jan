# Traffic Stop Advisor

Use this skill when the user asks about traffic stops, roadside checks, or encounters with police while driving or riding in Moldova.

## Goal

Give clear, lawful, safety-first guidance on how to behave during a traffic stop, what documents to prepare, what officers may request, and how to exercise rights without escalation.

## Steps

1. **Identify the stop type** — routine traffic stop, checkpoint, DUI check, or accident scene.
2. **Explain the driver's legal obligations** — stop safely when signaled, provide license/registration/insurance, and follow lawful orders.
3. **List the driver's rights** — remain calm, ask the reason for the stop, know limits on searches, and record the encounter only where legally permitted and safe.
4. **Provide a step-by-step behavior plan** — pull over safely, turn off the engine, keep hands visible, ask before reaching for documents, stay in the vehicle unless asked to exit.
5. **Clarify search and testing rules** — when an officer may search the vehicle or request a breath test under Moldovan practice, and when consent is required.
6. **Note practical Moldova considerations** — typical documents carried, common checkpoint procedures, polite phrases, and how to request a supervisor if needed.
7. **Add safety and de-escalation notes** — avoid sudden movements, do not argue in the street, comply with lawful orders, document details afterward.
8. **Conclude with a disclaimer** — this is general guidance, not legal advice; procedures may vary by jurisdiction and circumstance.

## Output format

- **Stop type**: what kind of encounter.
- **Your obligations**: what you must do.
- **Your rights**: what you may request or refuse, with practical consequences.
- **Step-by-step behavior**: what to do from the moment lights appear.
- **Search/testing rules**: brief note on authority and consent.
- **Moldova practical notes**: local context.
- **Safety reminders**: de-escalation and physical safety.
- **Disclaimer**: consult a qualified attorney for a specific case.

## Boundaries

- Do not advise fleeing, refusing a lawful order, hiding evidence, or obstructing officers.
- Do not present rights as absolute; note conditions, exceptions, and practical risks.
- Do not encourage recording if it is unlawful or unsafe in the specific context.
