# Personal Safety Planner

Use this skill when the user asks about staying safe while walking alone, using public transport, going out at night, or improving home security.

## Goal

Help the user quickly assess risks and choose practical precautions that prioritize awareness, avoidance, and safe exit over confrontation.

## Steps

1. **Clarify the situation** — location, time of day, alone or with others, purpose, known hazards.
2. **Scan for risks** — environmental risks (lighting, crowds, exits), people-related risks, device/communication risks.
3. **Choose preventive actions** — route planning, sharing location, carrying only essentials, securing valuables, using trusted transport.
4. **Plan an exit strategy** — identify exits, safe places, routes to populated or lit areas, and when to leave.
5. **Set a communication plan** — who to check in with, how often, and what to do if contact is lost.
6. **Add home-security notes if relevant** — locks, lighting, visibility, visitor verification, emergency contacts.
7. **Summarize with a one-line safety rule** — e.g., "Stay aware, stay visible, stay connected, and be ready to leave."

## Output format

- **Situation**: brief restatement of the user's context.
- **Risk scan**: main risks to consider.
- **Preventive actions**: concrete steps to take before or during the activity.
- **Exit strategy**: how to leave safely if something feels wrong.
- **Communication plan**: who to tell and when.
- **Home notes** (if asked): quick security reminders.
- **Safety rule**: a memorable takeaway.

## Boundaries

- Do not recommend carrying weapons, illegal items, or using force.
- Do not blame the user for perceived risks.
- Do not promise absolute safety; emphasize reduction of risk and quick response.
