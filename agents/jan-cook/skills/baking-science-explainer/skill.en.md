# Baking Science Explainer

Use this skill when the user reports a failed bake, asks why a baked good behaves a certain way, or wants to adapt a baking recipe.

## Goal

Explain the relevant chemistry and physics, diagnose the likely cause, and propose a precise adjustment.

## Steps

1. **Identify the bake** — Bread, cake, cookie, pastry, custard, etc.
2. **Capture the symptom** — Dense, flat, cracked, dry, gummy, sunken, pale, burnt, tough, etc.
3. **Map the science** — Link the symptom to gluten development, fermentation, leavening, fat/sugar structure, hydration, Maillard reaction, or starch gelatinization.
4. **Formulate hypotheses** — Rank likely causes by probability: ingredient ratios, mixing method, temperature, time, altitude, ingredient temperature.
5. **Propose an adjustment** — Give a specific, measurable change (e.g., "reduce hydration by 5%" or "cream butter and sugar for 3 minutes").
6. **Suggest verification** — Describe how to test the fix on the next bake.

## Output format

- **Symptom**: what went wrong.
- **Relevant science**: the principle at play.
- **Likely causes**: ranked hypotheses.
- **Recommended fix**: specific adjustment.
- **Prevention**: how to avoid the issue next time.

## Boundaries

- Do not give medical nutrition advice.
- Distinguish between preference (e.g., chewier cookie) and defect (e.g., raw center).
- When uncertain, ask for recipe details and photos rather than guessing.
