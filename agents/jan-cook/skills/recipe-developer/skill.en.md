# Recipe Developer

Use this skill when the user asks for a new recipe, wants to adapt an existing one, has a set of ingredients to use, or needs a dish matched to dietary constraints, cuisine, or serving size.

## Goal

Move from a vague cooking goal to a complete, reproducible recipe with clear measurements, timings, and technique notes.

## Steps

1. **Clarify the request** — Confirm cuisine, course, dietary restrictions, available equipment, skill level, and number of servings.
2. **Choose the approach** — Select an authentic technique or dish origin; do not present fusion as authentic.
3. **Build the ingredient list** — Use gram/milliliter measurements where possible; note ratios for scaling.
4. **Define timing and temperatures** — Provide prep time, cook time, resting/rising time, and internal temperatures for proteins.
5. **Specify equipment** — Call out pan material, oven type, thermometer, or specialty tools.
6. **Write the steps** — Use active verbs; explain the why behind critical steps (e.g., "sear to develop fond").
7. **Add notes and substitutions** — Mark substitutions that affect taste vs texture vs safety.
8. **Flag failure points** — Highlight overmixing, wrong pan material, altitude adjustments, or overcooking risks.

## Output format

- **Yield**: number of servings.
- **Prep time**: active preparation time.
- **Cook time**: active cooking/baking time.
- **Ingredients**: list with weights/volumes.
- **Equipment**: pans, tools, thermometers.
- **Steps**: numbered, technique-focused.
- **Notes**: substitutions, scaling, origins, failure points.

## Boundaries

- Do not guess safe cooking temperatures for meat, poultry, or seafood; cite standard internal temperatures.
- Keep advice within general nutrition guidelines; avoid medical nutrition claims.
- Respect dietary restrictions without judgment.
