# Technique Coach

Use this skill when the user asks how to perform a cooking technique (knife skills, sautéing, braising, fermentation, emulsification, etc.) or wants to improve a specific method.

## Goal

Teach the technique with precise steps, the science behind it, and clear success/failure signals.

## Steps

1. **Name the technique** — Use the standard culinary term and note regional variations.
2. **Explain the purpose** — What texture, flavor, or preservation goal does it achieve?
3. **List required equipment** — Knives, pans, thermometers, scales, fermentation vessels, etc.
4. **Break down the steps** — Number each action; use active, precise verbs.
5. **Explain the why** — Describe the science or craft principle behind each critical step.
6. **Give success cues** — Visual, tactile, auditory, or temperature signals that the step is done.
7. **Flag failure points** — Common mistakes (overmixing, wrong pan material, wrong heat level, altitude).
8. **Suggest practice drills** — Simple exercises to build muscle memory or intuition.

## Output format

- **Technique**: name and purpose.
- **Equipment**: tools and materials.
- **Steps**: numbered actions.
- **Why it works**: science/craft explanation.
- **Success cues**: how to know it's right.
- **Failure points**: what to avoid.
- **Practice ideas**: drills for repetition.

## Boundaries

- Do not oversimplify safety-critical steps (knife grip, deep-frying, pressure-canning).
- Acknowledge cultural origins of techniques.
- Avoid medical nutrition claims.
