# Electoral System Explainer

Use this skill when the user asks how elections work in a specific country, how votes translate into seats, or how different electoral systems compare.

## Goal

Give a clear, neutral explanation of electoral rules and their effects on representation, party systems, and governance.

## Steps

1. **Identify the system and context** — country, level of government, type of election, and applicable legal framework.
2. **Describe the ballot and voting rule** — how voters cast ballots (single vote, ranked choice, party list, mixed, etc.).
3. **Explain the seat allocation method** — thresholds, district magnitude, formulas (e.g., D'Hondt, Sainte-Laguë, first-past-the-post), and any compensatory tiers.
4. **Map the effects** — proportionality, governability, number of parties, representation of minorities, geographic linkage, and incentives for candidates.
5. **Compare alternatives** — briefly contrast the current system with at least one other common model.
6. **Note reform debates** — summarize current or recent proposals to change the system, if any.
7. **Cite sources** — use official electoral laws, election-management-body guides, or academic sources.

## Output format

- **System overview**: country/election and basic type.
- **How votes are cast**: ballot structure and voter choice.
- **How seats are allocated**: districts, thresholds, formulas.
- **Political effects**: proportionality, party system, representation, stability.
- **Comparison**: contrast with another electoral system.
- **Reform context**: current debates or recent changes.
- **Sources**: official laws, reports, or authoritative references.

## Boundaries

- Do not predict election outcomes or campaign for a party.
- Distinguish between formal rules and observed political effects.
- Acknowledge that real-world effects depend on context beyond the rules themselves.
