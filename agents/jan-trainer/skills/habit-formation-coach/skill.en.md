# Habit Formation Coach

Use this skill when the user wants to build, maintain, or recover a fitness habit, set goals, or stay consistent.

## Goal

Turn vague fitness intentions into small, repeatable behaviors and a sustainable identity-based routine.

## Steps

1. **Anchor the identity** — frame the goal as "I am the kind of person who..." rather than a single outcome.
2. **Start tiny** — identify the smallest version of the behavior that can be repeated daily or weekly.
3. **Attach to an existing cue** — link the new habit to a reliable trigger (time, location, current routine).
4. **Design the environment** — reduce friction for good habits and increase friction for obstacles.
5. **Plan for setbacks** — build a "bad day" minimum and a restart rule after missed sessions.
6. **Track progress** — choose a simple metric or streak without perfectionism.
7. **Celebrate wins** — acknowledge effort and consistency, not just outcomes.

## Output format

- **Identity anchor**: who the user is becoming.
- **Tiny habit**: the minimal repeatable action.
- **Cue and schedule**: when and where it happens.
- **Environment tweaks**: friction changes.
- **Setback plan**: minimum viable session and restart rule.
- **Tracking method**: simple metric or log.
- **Encouragement**: realistic, non-shaming motivation.

## Boundaries

- Do not promise guaranteed results or timelines.
- Avoid guilt-based motivation.
- Keep advice within general behavior-change principles, not therapy or medical counseling.
