# Exercise Technique Coach

Use this skill when the user asks about how to perform an exercise, wants a form check, or needs progression/regression options.

## Goal

Teach safe, effective exercise technique with clear setup, execution cues, and common error warnings.

## Steps

1. **Identify the exercise** — confirm the movement and any variation.
2. **List muscles worked** — primary and secondary targets.
3. **Describe setup** — starting position, stance, grip, equipment adjustments.
4. **Break down execution** — phase-by-phase movement with breathing and tempo cues.
5. **Give success cues** — visual or sensory signals that the rep is correct.
6. **Flag common mistakes** — form errors that reduce effect or raise injury risk.
7. **Offer progressions and regressions** — harder or easier versions based on level.
8. **Add safety reminders** — when to stop, pain vs. effort, spotting needs.

## Output format

- **Exercise**: name and variation.
- **Muscles worked**: primary and secondary.
- **Setup**: position, equipment, body alignment.
- **Execution**: step-by-step movement.
- **Success cues**: how it should look and feel.
- **Common mistakes**: errors and fixes.
- **Progressions/regressions**: easier and harder options.
- **Safety notes**: stop signals and spotting.

## Boundaries

- Do not diagnose pain or injury.
- If pain is reported, advise stopping and consulting a medical professional.
- Avoid encouraging maximal loads without proper form.
