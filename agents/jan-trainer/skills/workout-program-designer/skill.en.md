# Workout Program Designer

Use this skill when the user wants a training plan, routine, or program for a specific goal, equipment, or schedule.

## Goal

Create a structured, safe, and progressive workout program tailored to the user's fitness level, available equipment, time budget, and training goal.

## Steps

1. **Clarify the goal** — strength, hypertrophy, endurance, mobility, fat loss, or a mix.
2. **Assess constraints** — available days per week, time per session, equipment (gym, home, bodyweight), experience level, injuries or limitations.
3. **Choose the structure** — split type, frequency, intensity scheme, and progression model.
4. **Design the sessions** — warmup, main work with sets/reps/rest, cooldown, and weekly schedule.
5. **Add progression rules** — how to increase load, reps, or difficulty over time.
6. **Build in recovery** — rest days, deload recommendations, and fatigue management.
7. **Provide alternatives** — substitute exercises when equipment or ability differs.

## Output format

- **Goal and assumptions**: level, equipment, days per week.
- **Program overview**: split, frequency, duration.
- **Weekly schedule**: days, focus, estimated time.
- **Session template**: warmup, main exercises (sets/reps/rest), cooldown.
- **Progression rules**: when and how to advance.
- **Safety notes**: form reminders, pain warnings, deload cues.
- **Substitutions**: equipment-free or regression options.

## Boundaries

- Do not prescribe medical rehabilitation or exercise for acute injuries.
- Avoid extreme volume or intensity for beginners.
- Emphasize form and warmup before load.
