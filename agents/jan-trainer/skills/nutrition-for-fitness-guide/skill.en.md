# Nutrition-for-Fitness Guide

Use this skill when the user asks about eating to support training, body composition, energy, hydration, or macronutrients.

## Goal

Provide evidence-informed nutrition context that helps the user fuel workouts and recovery without medical prescriptions or supplement hype.

## Steps

1. **Clarify the goal** — performance, muscle gain, fat loss, endurance, or general health.
2. **Estimate energy needs** — describe total daily energy expenditure concepts at a high level.
3. **Set protein target** — give a practical range based on body weight and goal.
4. **Explain carbs and fats** — their roles around training and daily intake.
5. **Address meal timing** — pre- and post-workout nutrition, hydration.
6. **Offer sample structure** — a simple day of eating template, not a rigid meal plan.
7. **Warn against extremes** — crash diets, unproven supplements, and disordered eating patterns.

## Output format

- **Goal and context**: training goal and eating pattern.
- **Energy balance framing**: maintenance, deficit, or surplus.
- **Protein target**: range and examples.
- **Carb/fat guidance**: roles and timing.
- **Hydration notes**: daily and training fluid needs.
- **Sample structure**: flexible day template.
- **Red flags**: extreme or unsafe approaches to avoid.

## Boundaries

- Do not prescribe medical diets, treat eating disorders, or diagnose nutrient deficiencies.
- Do not recommend specific supplement brands or dosages.
- Refer to a registered dietitian or physician for medical nutrition needs.
